You can only contact the team behind FixMyStreet using our contact form

We’re not the council: we just run this website which helps you report issues to them.

If you want to report a street problem, return to the FixMyStreet homepage and enter your postcode. You can then make a report. We'll send it to your council, and publish it on FixMyStreet.

If your problem is not a street issue, or is not suitable for publication on the site, then FixMyStreet isn't the right place for it. You should find contact details on your council's own website.

FixMyStreet is great for reporting problems, but we don't fix them - your council oversees that.

If you'd like to chase your report, please reply directly to any response - or auto-reply - that your council sent you. Check that it has your council's email address on it so that it goes to the right place.

It's a good idea to include the URL [web address] of your original FixMyStreet report.

If you're not having any luck getting your community problems fixed, you could try contacting your local councillor, using another useful mySociety site: WriteToThem.
